FYP Project - Understanding the impact of noise pollution on human health

The purpose of the research project is to study noise pollution in Singapore and understand how it affects the mental and physical health in humans. Noise pollution is defined as “regular exposure to elevated sound levels that may lead to adverse effects in humans”. The first part of the research will analyze the common noises found in Singapore to determine the sound that is the dominant. Audio files will be shown and the following questions will be asked. Please keep these definitions in mind when answering the question.


Question Definitions
Please select the dominant noise you hear in the clip. Dominant Noise - The noise that stands out to you in the clip
Please select whether the noise is pleasant, unpleasant or neutral. Pleasant - A noise that invokes a positive emotion in oneself
Unpleasant - A noise that invokes a negative emotion in oneself
Neutral - A noise that invokes neither positive or negative emotions in oneself


Right to Refuse or Withdraw:

Your participation in this study is voluntary and you may choose not to participate. There is no penalty if you refuse to participate or withdraw from the study at any time.

Risks and Discomforts:

There are no anticipated risks to human subjects other than those common to daily life.

Anonymous and Confidential Data Collection:

The information you provide will be kept strictly anonymous and confidential.

Confidentiality of records:

Your demographic details, such as your age and gender and ethnicity will be collected. However, to protect your confidentiality, this information will not be attached to your responses in the survey. Finally, your survey response will be used for research purposes only. All data are to be stored in a password protected electronic format. Only the researchers for this study will have access to the records.
